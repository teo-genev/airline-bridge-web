Time's Factor

Timing Is Key

Booking your flight too early or too late often results in higher prices. If you purchase months in advance or wait until the last minute, airlines assume you’re locked into those dates and will charge accordingly. Research suggests the sweet spot for booking is around two months before departure for the best deals.

Season Factor

High and Low Seasons

Airlines typically operate on a summer (April - October) and winter (November - March) schedule. Prices tend to soar during the summer due to higher demand, while winter months often bring lower fares as fewer people are traveling. If you’re looking for cheaper flights, aim to book during the off-season.

Time of Day

Time of Day Matters

Early morning and late-night flights are generally more affordable. Most travelers prefer mid-day flights, making those times pricier. So, if you’re willing to wake up early or fly overnight, you can often snag a better deal.
